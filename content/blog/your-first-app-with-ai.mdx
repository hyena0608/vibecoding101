## You Can Build an App Today

This isn't hype. By the end of this guide, you'll understand exactly how to go from idea to a live app on the internet — without writing a single line of code yourself.

## The 5-Step Process

### Step 1: Start with a Clear Idea

The most important skill in vibe coding isn't technical — it's being able to describe what you want clearly.

**Bad prompt:** "Make me an app"

**Good prompt:** "Build a personal finance tracker where I can add expenses with a category, amount, and date. Show a monthly summary with a pie chart of spending by category."

<Callout type="tip">
Write your app description before opening any tool. Think about: What does it do? Who uses it? What are the main features?
</Callout>

### Step 2: Choose Your Tool

For your first app, we recommend:

- **Simple landing page or form** → Use [v0](https://v0.dev)
- **App with data storage** → Use [Bolt.new](https://bolt.new) or [Cursor](https://cursor.sh)
- **Complex multi-page app** → Use [Cursor](https://cursor.sh) or [Claude Code](https://claude.ai)

### Step 3: Describe and Iterate

Start with your core description, then refine:

1. Give your main description
2. Review what AI generates
3. Ask for specific changes: "Make the header blue," "Add a search bar," "The button should be larger"
4. Repeat until it looks right

<Callout type="info">
Don't try to describe everything in one prompt. Start simple and add features one at a time. This is called "iterative development" and it's how professional developers work too.
</Callout>

### Step 4: Test It

Before sharing your app:

- Click every button — does it do what you expect?
- Try entering weird data — does it handle errors gracefully?
- Check it on your phone — does it look good on small screens?

If something's broken, describe the issue to the AI: "When I click the submit button with an empty form, it crashes. Add validation that shows an error message instead."

### Step 5: Deploy It

Deploying means putting your app on the internet so others can use it. Most AI tools make this easy:

- **v0**: Click "Deploy" to get a live URL instantly
- **Bolt.new**: Built-in deploy to Netlify
- **Cursor + Vercel**: Push to GitHub, Vercel auto-deploys

Your app is now live. Share the URL with anyone in the world.

## Common Mistakes Beginners Make

1. **Too vague** — "Make it look good" vs. "Use a clean, modern design with lots of whitespace and a blue accent color"
2. **Too much at once** — Building everything in one prompt vs. building feature by feature
3. **Ignoring errors** — When something breaks, describe the exact issue to the AI
4. **Not testing** — Always test your app before sharing it

## Real Example: Building a Recipe Saver

Here's a real session that took 15 minutes:

> **Prompt 1:** "Build a recipe saver app. I want to add recipes with a name, ingredients list, and instructions. Show all my recipes in a card grid."
>
> **Prompt 2:** "Add a search bar at the top that filters recipes by name."
>
> **Prompt 3:** "Each recipe card should show the recipe name, number of ingredients, and a colored tag for the category (breakfast, lunch, dinner, snack)."
>
> **Prompt 4:** "Make it save recipes to local storage so they persist when I refresh the page."

Four prompts. 15 minutes. A fully working recipe app.
